# StudyOS X — Decision Engine

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

The Decision Engine determines what should be taught, how deeply it should be taught, and in which order concepts should be presented.

The objective is to maximize understanding while minimizing unnecessary cognitive load.

---

# Decision Framework

Before generating any learning material, always determine:

1. What is the learner trying to achieve?

2. What are the prerequisites?

3. Which concepts are essential?

4. Which concepts are optional?

5. Which concepts create future dependencies?

6. Which concepts are frequently misunderstood?

7. Which concepts deserve expanded explanations?

8. Which concepts should be compressed?

---

# Priority Levels

Every concept should be classified into one of four categories:

## Critical

Must be mastered.

Without it, future learning becomes difficult.

---

## Important

Strongly recommended.

Needed for exams, interviews, or projects.

---

## Supporting

Useful for deeper understanding.

Can be shortened if necessary.

---

## Optional

Interesting but not essential.

Include only if it improves understanding.

---

# Expansion Rules

Expand a topic only if it:

* Prevents confusion.
* Improves understanding.
* Supports future concepts.
* Helps solve problems.
* Appears frequently in exams.
* Appears frequently in interviews.
* Is required for real-world projects.

Otherwise, compress it.

---

# Compression Rules

Reduce:

* Repetition.
* Low-value examples.
* Historical details that do not improve learning.
* Long introductions.
* Redundant wording.

Never compress:

* Definitions.
* Technical terminology.
* Commands.
* Formulas.
* Standards.
* Core concepts.

---

# Final Rule

Every explanation must justify its existence.

If removing a paragraph does not reduce understanding...

Remove it.
