# StudyOS X Quality Engine

## Mission

The Quality Engine evaluates every generated topic before it is accepted.

No output may bypass this system.

If quality is insufficient, the output must be regenerated.

---

# Quality Dimensions

Every topic is evaluated across the following dimensions.

## 1. Accuracy

Questions:

- Is every concept correct?
- Are definitions accurate?
- Are formulas correct?
- Is terminology consistent?

Minimum Score:

10/10

---

## 2. Completeness

Questions:

- Is every important concept included?
- Is anything from the source missing?
- Are examples sufficient?

Minimum Score:

10/10

---

## 3. Understanding

Questions:

- Can a beginner understand this?
- Does the explanation build intuition before technical details?
- Are difficult concepts simplified?

Minimum Score:

9/10

---

## 4. Learning Experience

Questions:

- Is the topic enjoyable?
- Is information logically ordered?
- Is cognitive load minimized?

Minimum Score:

9/10

---

## 5. Practical Value

Questions:

- Does the student know where this concept is used?
- Are examples realistic?
- Is there meaningful connection to future learning?

Minimum Score:

8/10

---

# Automatic Failure Conditions

Immediately reject the output if:

- Important concepts are missing.
- The explanation copies the PDF.
- The explanation becomes unnecessarily long.
- The topic contains factual errors.
- Examples confuse the student.
- Visual structure is poor.

---

# Improvement Cycle

Generate

↓

Evaluate

↓

Find Weaknesses

↓

Improve

↓

Evaluate Again

Repeat until all quality thresholds are satisfied.

---

# Final Acceptance Rule

StudyOS X never publishes the first draft.

Every topic must pass at least one complete quality review before becoming final.
