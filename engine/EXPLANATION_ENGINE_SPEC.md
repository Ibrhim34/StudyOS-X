# Explanation Engine Specification

## Purpose

The Explanation Engine transforms a **Teaching Blueprint** into high-quality educational explanations.

It is responsible for teaching concepts in a way that maximizes understanding, not memorization.

The engine never analyzes the original PDF and never extracts knowledge directly.

It only consumes the Teaching Blueprint.

---

# Input

* Teaching Blueprint

---

# Output

A complete educational explanation for one learning topic.

---

# Responsibilities

The Explanation Engine must generate:

1. Learning Goal
2. Big Picture
3. Intuition
4. Formal Explanation
5. Deep Explanation
6. Connections to Previous Knowledge
7. Real-world Importance
8. Transition to the Next Topic

---

# Processing Logic

For every topic:

Step 1

Understand the learner profile.

↓

Step 2

Read the Teaching Blueprint.

↓

Step 3

Determine the explanation depth.

↓

Step 4

Generate intuition before formal definitions.

↓

Step 5

Introduce formal concepts.

↓

Step 6

Expand using deeper reasoning.

↓

Step 7

Connect the topic to previous concepts.

↓

Step 8

Prepare the learner for the next topic.

---

# Teaching Rules

Always:

* Teach before defining.
* Explain before proving.
* Build intuition before mathematics.
* Connect every new idea to existing knowledge.
* Use progressively deeper explanations.
* Reduce unnecessary cognitive load.

Never:

* Copy textbook wording.
* Jump directly into formulas.
* Introduce undefined terminology.
* Assume prior knowledge that is not in the Teaching Blueprint.

---

# Explanation Levels

The engine must adapt explanations according to the learner profile.

## Beginner

* Simple language.
* Everyday analogies.
* Minimal mathematics.

---

## Intermediate

* Academic language.
* Moderate depth.
* Practical reasoning.

---

## Advanced

* Complete technical explanations.
* Mathematical reasoning.
* Edge cases.
* Professional terminology.

---

# Output Structure

Each generated explanation must follow this order:

1. Learning Goal

2. Big Picture

3. Intuition

4. Formal Explanation

5. Deep Explanation

6. Why It Matters

7. Connection to Previous Topic

8. Transition to Next Topic

---

# Validation Rules

A generated explanation is valid only if:

* Learning Goal exists.
* Big Picture exists.
* Intuition exists.
* Formal Explanation exists.
* Deep Explanation exists.
* Logical transitions exist.
* The explanation matches the learner profile.

---

# Failure Conditions

Reject the explanation if:

* It skips prerequisites.
* It starts with formulas.
* It copies textbook text.
* It introduces unexplained terminology.
* It cannot be understood independently.

---

# Success Criteria

A learner should be able to understand the topic after one careful reading without returning to the original PDF.

The explanation should feel like it was delivered by an excellent instructor rather than generated by an AI.
