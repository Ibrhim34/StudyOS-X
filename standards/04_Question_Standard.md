# StudyOS X Question Standard

## Purpose

This standard defines how assessment questions are designed in StudyOS X.

Questions are used to measure understanding, not memorization.

---

# Core Principle

A good question reveals how well the student understands a concept.

It should encourage reasoning rather than recalling sentences.

---

# Learning Levels

Questions should progressively evaluate:

1. Recall
2. Understanding
3. Application
4. Analysis
5. Evaluation
6. Creation (when appropriate)

---

# Question Types

## Recall

Verify key definitions or facts.

Use sparingly.

---

## Conceptual

Ask the student to explain an idea in their own words.

Preferred over pure memorization.

---

## Application

Require using the concept in a realistic situation.

---

## Analytical

Require comparing, interpreting or reasoning.

---

## Scenario-Based

Present a real-world problem and ask the learner to apply the concept.

---

# Difficulty Distribution

A balanced topic should approximately contain:

- 20% Easy
- 50% Medium
- 30% Challenging

---

# Feedback Rules

Whenever possible, provide:

- Correct answer
- Explanation
- Why incorrect options are wrong

Questions should also teach.

---

# Things to Avoid

Never ask questions that:

- Depend on memorizing wording.
- Are intentionally misleading.
- Test knowledge that has not been taught.
- Have ambiguous answers.

---

# Success Criteria

A student who answers correctly should demonstrate understanding, not repetition.
